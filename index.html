How I think

Reading, synthesis, and tools for thought

I use reading, note systems, and sketching to turn scattered ideas into usable structure. Forecasting, speculative fiction, design, and systems thinking all show up here as ways of training attention.

  • Reading trails that sharpen how I frame uncertainty and systems behavior.
  • Notes and drawings that turn abstract ideas into reusable shapes.
  • Experiments with tools that help context survive collaboration.

What I build

Decision systems, ML workflows, and calmer planning structures

The technical layer matters, but the deeper interest is making a system legible enough that people will trust it, question it, and keep using it after the first launch.

  • Forecasting routines that convert noisy signals into planning inputs.
  • Human-in-the-loop AI workflows that support review and accountability.
  • Operational artifacts that reduce coordination drag and preserve context.

How I work

Human-centered, pragmatic, and built for real teams

I like problems where the hard part is not only analysis or implementation, but helping a group see the same picture at the same time. The systems I care about need to survive handoffs, ambiguity, and change.

  • Design for judgment, not just automation.
  • Favor rhythms and artifacts people can actually maintain.
  • Leave behind structures that make future decisions easier.
